Consumer Behaviour & Market Strategy

GoPro × L’Oréal — Action-Proof Makeup

Project Overview

This project explored a hypothetical collaboration between GoPro and L’Oréal to design an Action-Proof Makeup Line for women with active lifestyles.

The study combined market analysis, consumer behaviour research, product concept development, and marketing strategy design to understand how beauty products can perform under real-life conditions such as heat, sweat, travel, and outdoor activities.

A key component of the project was primary consumer research through qualitative interviews, where insights from different age groups were analysed to understand attitudes toward long-lasting, performance-based beauty products.

The concept positioned makeup not just as a beauty product, but as a functional lifestyle tool for modern women balancing work, fitness, travel, and content creation.

Objective

The project aimed to:

  • Study consumer behaviour in the beauty industry, particularly among women with active lifestyles
  • Explore the market potential of performance-based makeup products
  • Conduct primary consumer research through interviews across different age groups
  • Analyse behavioural motivations influencing beauty purchasing decisions
  • Develop a product concept and marketing strategy aligned with modern lifestyle trends

Research & Strategy

Market & Consumer Analysis

Market research revealed a growing demand for long-lasting, sweat-resistant, and travel-friendly beauty products, especially among women who engage in fitness, outdoor activities, and digital content creation.

Key market observations included:

  • Rise of “active beauty” and performance-based cosmetics
  • Increasing participation of women in travel, fitness, and outdoor activities
  • Social media and content creation influencing beauty consumption
  • Demand for compact, multifunctional products suitable for busy lifestyles

Consumers increasingly expect makeup to perform in real-life conditions rather than controlled studio environments.

Primary Research — Consumer Interviews

A major component of the project involved qualitative consumer interviews (Sample size: 12 respondents). Age groups studied included Gen Z students (16-22), young professionals (23-30), and working professionals (31-40).

The interviews explored consumer reactions to the concept, interest in sweat-proof makeup, and lifestyle compatibility. The responses were analysed individually to identify behavioural patterns across age groups and lifestyles.

Consumer Behaviour Insights

Analysis of the interviews revealed several key behavioural insights:

  • Functional Motivation: Consumers prefer products that stay intact during heat, sweat, and long days, reducing the need for frequent touch-ups.
  • Emotional Motivation: Performance-based makeup increases confidence in social situations, travel environments, and content creation scenarios.
  • Social Influence: Beauty influencers, peer recommendations, and real-life performance tests significantly impact purchase decisions.
  • Lifestyle Fit: Women with active routines prefer compact, travel-friendly products that combine convenience with durability.

Concept Development

Product Concept

The project proposed an Action-Proof Makeup Collection designed specifically for real-world conditions. Key ideas included a Sweat-Proof Foundation Stick, Waterproof Tint Stick, Humidity-Proof Compact, All-Day Setting Spray, and a Travel Mini Kit.

The concept emphasized durability during heat and movement, compact travel packaging, and performance testing in real-life scenarios.

Marketing Strategy & Identity

The marketing strategy focused on positioning the product at the intersection of beauty, performance, and lifestyle culture.

  • Fitness Culture: Sweat-test challenges, gym collaborations, athlete endorsements.
  • Travel & Adventure: Outdoor beauty testing content, travel creator partnerships, airport retail pop-ups.
  • Creator Economy: GRWM outdoor content, POV beauty testing videos, creator travel kits.

As part of the project, a collaboration logo concept was designed combining GoPro’s action camera symbolism with L’Oréal’s beauty identity.

Key Outcome

The project demonstrated strong potential for a performance-based cosmetics category designed for modern active lifestyles. Consumer interviews showed clear interest in products that combine durability, portability, real-life performance, and lifestyle compatibility.
