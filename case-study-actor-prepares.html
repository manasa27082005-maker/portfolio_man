Social Media Analysis Project

Actor Prepares — Social Media Case Study

Project Overview

This project involved a real-world case study analyzing the social media strategy and digital presence of Actor Prepares Academy.

The study combined primary research, social listening analytics, and competitor benchmarking to understand how performing arts institutions manage their digital communication and audience engagement.

The project examined online mentions, sentiment trends, engagement patterns, and brand positioning, while also incorporating expert insights from a former Social Media Manager of the academy.

Objective

The project aimed to:

  • Analyze Actor Prepares’ social media presence and audience engagement patterns
  • Understand how performing arts institutions manage digital communication
  • Conduct a 365-day social listening analysis to study brand mentions and sentiment
  • Compare Actor Prepares with a competitor institution to evaluate digital visibility
  • Generate strategic insights that could improve the academy’s digital marketing approach

Research & Analysis

Primary Research — Industry Interview

Conducted an in-depth interview with Isha Ingle, former Social Media Manager at Actor Prepares, to understand real-world social media management practices.

The discussion explored content strategy, engagement techniques, the role of trends (Reels, interactive content), audience behaviour, and planning workflows. The interview revealed that short-form video content, interactive posts, and emotion-driven storytelling tend to generate the strongest engagement for performing arts institutions.

Social Listening Analysis

Using Brand24, I conducted a 365-day digital listening analysis to examine online conversations around Actor Prepares. Key metrics identified included 65 total brand mentions, 48K social media reach, and 691K non-social reach. Positive mentions heavily outweighed negative (9 positive vs 1 negative), indicating strong brand sentiment. 31 pieces of user-generated content were found.

Competitor Benchmarking

A comparative media analysis was conducted between Actor Prepares and the National School of Drama to understand relative digital presence across mentions, reach, engagement, sentiment, and user-generated content.

The comparison showed that while Actor Prepares has strong brand sentiment, National School of Drama generates significantly higher online mentions and reach, highlighting opportunities for improved digital visibility.

Key Insights & Outcome

Key Insights

  • Interactive content and short-form video significantly increase audience engagement.
  • Emotional storytelling and behind-the-scenes content resonate strongly with creative audiences.
  • Social listening data can reveal visibility gaps compared to competing institutions.
  • A mix of organic content and paid campaigns is necessary for sustained digital growth.

Key Outcome

The project demonstrated how social listening analytics, primary research interviews, and competitor benchmarking can be combined to evaluate and improve an organization’s digital communication strategy. The insights generated from this study provide actionable directions for strengthening content strategy, engagement techniques, and digital positioning for performing arts institutions.
