ECOHAVEN — Digital Content Marketing Strategy

Project Overview

ECOHAVEN is a semester-long digital marketing project focused on developing a strategic communication and content marketing plan for a hypothetical vegan and sustainable fashion brand. The project explored how eco-conscious clothing brands can attract, engage, and retain digital audiences through structured content strategies and social media marketing.

The project was executed in three phases, combining brand strategy development, audience research, social media listening, and practical digital marketing execution.

The goal was to demonstrate how a sustainability-focused fashion brand could build a strong digital presence while addressing key consumer concerns around ethical fashion, affordability, and transparency.

View Full Project Document

Objective

The project aimed to:

  • Develop a digital marketing strategy for a vegan and sustainable clothing brand
  • Identify key audience segments interested in ethical fashion
  • Understand online conversations around sustainable clothing brands
  • Design content strategies across the TOFU–MOFU–BOFU marketing funnel
  • Execute selected strategies to demonstrate practical digital marketing implementation

Phases & Execution

Phase 1 — Brand & Digital Strategy Development

The first phase focused on building the brand foundation and digital positioning for ECOHAVEN.

Key activities included:

  • Developing the brand concept for a minimalist vegan fashion label
  • Analyzing market opportunities for sustainable clothing in India
  • Conducting competitor research within the ethical fashion space
  • Designing a TOFU–MOFU–BOFU content marketing funnel
  • Planning digital strategies including Instagram content, Pinterest lookbooks, SEO blogs, and influencer collaborations

This phase established ECOHAVEN’s positioning as an affordable, stylish, and transparent vegan fashion brand targeting conscious consumers. The brand identity emphasized minimal aesthetics, gender-neutral silhouettes, and plant-based fabrics such as organic cotton and hemp blends.

Phase 2 — Audience Personas & Social Media Listening

The second phase focused on understanding the target audience and analyzing online conversations around sustainable fashion.

Audience Persona Development:

  • Ananya Nair — The Idealist: A socially conscious college student interested in trendy yet affordable sustainable fashion.
  • Riya Singh — The Rational Consumer: A working professional seeking minimalist, high-quality clothing aligned with sustainability values.
  • Kabir Mehta — The Explorer: A sustainability-driven lifestyle enthusiast who prefers durable, ethically produced clothing.

Social Media Listening:

Social listening was conducted using BrandMentions to analyze conversations around sustainable fashion brands. Key insights revealed that Instagram is the dominant platform, consumers strongly value ethical production and eco-friendly fabrics, and key concerns include pricing, trend relevance, and brand transparency.

Phase 3 — Strategy Execution

The final phase involved implementing selected strategies from the marketing funnel to demonstrate practical digital marketing execution. From the strategies proposed in earlier phases, two strategies from each stage of the funnel (TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU) were selected for execution.

  • TOFU (Awareness & Discovery): Pinterest lookbook boards showcasing eco-friendly outfit inspiration and sustainable styling, and educational blog content explaining sustainable fabrics.
  • MOFU (Consideration & Trust): Fabric comparison content explaining sustainability differences between materials like hemp and cotton, and customer testimonial content demonstrating social proof.
  • BOFU (Retention & Advocacy): Post-purchase thank-you email concepts reinforcing the brand’s sustainability mission and referral campaign concepts encouraging customers to promote the brand.

Research Insights & Strategy

Key insights from the research process included:

  • Rising consumer awareness of sustainable and ethical fashion
  • Gen Z and young professionals emerging as key eco-conscious consumers
  • Instagram and Pinterest acting as major fashion discovery platforms
  • Consumers value sustainability but are concerned about pricing and trend relevance

The digital strategy focused on:

  • Educational content around sustainable fabrics and ethical fashion
  • Visual storytelling through Instagram and Pinterest
  • Community engagement through user-generated content
  • Transparent communication around sourcing and sustainability

Key Outcome

The project resulted in a structured digital marketing strategy for a sustainable fashion brand, supported by audience research, competitor analysis, and practical content execution examples. By combining persona insights, social listening data, and funnel-based strategies, ECOHAVEN was positioned as a modern eco-conscious brand capable of connecting with sustainability-driven consumers online.
